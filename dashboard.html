Shipwaves Dashboard

Overview

Shipwaves users currently manage their workflow by switching between dedicated modules. While each module is functional, the lack of a consolidated view leads to inefficiencies and potential oversight.

The Problem

Existing Shipwaves platform users lacked a central dashboard, forcing inefficient switching between separate shipment, document and billing modules to understand their operational status. This fragmented workflow made it difficult to get a quick overview, prioritize tasks effectively and identify critical alerts scattered across different sections, leading to wasted time and potential oversights.

The Goal

The primary goal of this project was to design and introduce a central dashboard that would serve as the main hub for users. Specific objectives included:

  • Consolidate Key Information: Provide a unified view of critical data points like active shipments, pending tasks, alerts, and key performance indicators (KPIs).
  • Improve Efficiency: Reduce the time and effort required to monitor operations and access relevant modules.
  • Enhance User Experience: Create a more intuitive, proactive, and satisfying user experience.
Research & Discovery

We conducted interviews with 3 Operations Managers. We observed their existing workflows, identified pain points related to information access and discussed what information they needed most readily available. Key questions included:

  • What information do you check first thing in the morning?
  • What are the most common tasks you jump between modules for?
  • What critical events require immediate attention?
  • How do you currently track performance?

Competitive Analysis: Reviewed dashboards from other logistics and SaaS platforms to understand common patterns, best practices, and potential innovations.

User Persona: Umesh Kamble is a 33-year-old male Operations Manager who prioritizes efficiency in his daily tasks. He requires immediate visibility into shipments that need his attention, proactive notifications regarding potential delays, and a straightforward method for creating new bookings. A significant pain point for Umesh is the current cumbersome process of navigating through multiple screens simply to track the status of urgent shipments, hindering his ability to respond quickly to critical situations.

Solution

The final design delivered a fully integrated RFQ module within the shipment management platform. Key features included:

  • A step-by-step wizard for shippers to create detailed RFQs with standardized fields.
  • Dashboards for shippers and carriers to manage and track RFQs and quotes effectively.
  • A dynamic quote comparison tool for shippers.
  • Seamless integration, allowing an awarded quote to potentially pre-populate data for booking a new shipment within the same platform.

This project highlighted the importance of understanding the distinct needs and workflows of different user groups (shippers vs. vendors) within the same feature. Balancing these needs while maintaining a cohesive experience was a key challenge. Early and frequent user testing was invaluable in identifying usability issues and validating design decisions.
